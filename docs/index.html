Solar Flare Forecast

This page shows a live daily forecast of whether a major (M or X class) solar flare will happen on the Sun in the next 24 hours. It updates on its own once a day, logs every forecast, and grades itself against what actually happened and against NOAA's official forecast. I built it to learn how this works, and the whole point was to keep it honest. The code and the full writeup are on GitHub.

Active-region data comes from NASA and Stanford JSOC, flare outcomes and the NOAA baseline from the NOAA Space Weather Prediction Center, and the training data from the SWAN-SF benchmark. On historical data the forecast scores an AUC of 0.94. On the live Sun it currently runs more conservative than NOAA, which this page shows honestly rather than hiding.
